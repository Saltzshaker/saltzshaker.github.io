writing

I have years of experience crafting content for and about products, writing on topics ranging from virtual reality to Bob Hope's dad jokes. Check out a few of my favorites.

Speech Recognition for Media: It’s Not All About Accuracy (PBS MediaShift)

A call for publishers to consider the benefits of imperfect machine-generated transcripts. Proud to have snuck in references to dada poetry, Nicki Minaj, and FDR all in the same article.

Reflection on Weird Reality: Beyond magic and novelty (Medium)

A quick idea dump about VR hype written in October 2016 following the Weird Reality Symposium.

What are the top challenges facing audio in 2015? (Medium, republished on PBS MediaShift)

A two-part collection of quotes from podcast and radio influencers surveying challenges facing spoken word audio in the digital landscape. Created in collaboration with Anne Wootton.

Digital doesn’t mean permanent (Medium)

A piece on the risks of digital archiving, and using Internet Archive to protect against “delete all.”

Bob Hope & Atomic Bill (Popcast Podcast)

I wrote the script for this podcast episode about "The Quick and the Dead," a 1950 NBC special about atomic energy hosted by Bob Hope.

Using speech technology for political analysis (Medium)

In this project the Pop Up Archive team took a look at how text analysis tools could bring insight about the speech patterns and key topics during an especially crowded Oakland mayoral race.

Combining humans and machines to describe audio (Pop Up Archive)

An articulation of the value and complexity of editable machine-generated tags. (Pardon the suboptimal newsletter formatting.)
